Format

SWISS STAGE

from November 28th to December 5th, 2024

Swiss System Format

Swiss System is a system where the teams with the same score will match each other. There will be a total of 5 rounds for this Swiss System. Teams that win 3 rounds will qualify for the playoffs stage. Teams that lose 3 rounds will be eliminated.

  • Round 1
    • The 16 teams will be randomly matched.
    • Matches will be Bo1.
  • Round 2
    • The 8 teams that have a 1-0 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • Matches will be Bo1.
    • The 8 teams that have a 0-1 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • Matches will be Bo1.
  • Round 3
    • The 4 teams that have a 2-0 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • The winner of this match will advance to the playoffs stage.
    • Matches will be Bo3.
    • The 8 teams that have a 1-1 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • Matches will be Bo1.
    • The 4 teams that have a 0-2 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • The loser of this match will be eliminated.
    • Matches will be Bo3.
  • Round 4
    • The 6 teams that have a 2-1 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • The winner of this match will advance to the playoffs stage.
    • Matches will be Bo3.
    • The 6 teams that have a 1-2 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • The loser of this match will be eliminated.
    • Matches will be Bo3.
  • Round 5
    • The 6 teams that have a 2-2 aggregate will be randomly matched.
    • The winner of this match will advance to the playoffs stage.
    • The loser of this match will be eliminated.
    • Matches will be Bo3.

PLAYOFFS

from December 7th to December 15th, 2024

Double Elimination Format

The Double Elimination Format is a system where a team must lose twice (other than Grand Finals) to be eliminated. The tournament consists of two main brackets:

  • Upper Bracket
    • All teams start in the Upper Bracket.
    • Teams that lose a match in this bracket is drop to the Lower Bracket.
    • Winners advance towards the Finals.
      • First Matches (Upper Bracket Quaterfinals) are played in a Bo3.
      • The rest are all played in Bo5.
  • Lower Bracket
    • Teams that lose in the Upper Bracket get a second chance in this bracket.
    • Teams losing a match in this bracket are eliminated from the tournament.
    • The last remaining team in the Losers' Bracket advances to the Grand Finals.
      • All matches are played in Bo5.
  • Grand Finals
    • The match will be played in a Bo7

What Makes M - Series so Significant

The M - Series is the most prestigious event in the world of MLBB esports, attracting the best teams from various regions to compete for the title of World Champion. The tournament is known for its high-level competition, exciting matches, and massive prize pools, making it a must-watch event for fans of the game.

In addition to the championship title, Moonton awards the winning team with a custom skin of their choice which marks their legacy in the history of MLBB.

Previous Winners

M1 World Championship (2019) - EVOS Legends

Champion Skin: Harith

The skin is chosen due to EVOS midlaner (Oura) exceptional performance that brought the white tigers back from a 3 - 1 deficit to win the first-ever M-Series World Championship.

M2 World Championship (2020) - Bren Esports

Bren Esports sought to seek their revenge against Burmese Gouls who knocked them down into the lower brackets in the upper Bracket Finals. After a thrilling neck-to-neck series, Bren Esports emerged victorious with a 4 - 3 scoreline.

Champion Skin: Lancelot

The skin is dedicated to Bren's jungler Karltzy who shown outstanding performance with his Lancelot who also claim the first and only Savage in the M2 World Championship.

M3 World Championship (2021) - Blacklist International

After a shocking defeat in the first round of the playoffs, Blacklist International made the legendary lower bracket to the Grand Finals and defeating their filipino counterpart ONIC PH with a 4 - 0 scoreline.

Champion Skin: Estes

The skin represents Blacklist International's identity as they revolutionised the MLBB by implementing Utility Junglers paired with support heroes like Estes, which is recognised as the UBE strat as part of Blacklist's identity. Estes also also happend to be Blacklist's roamer's (OhmyVeenus) most iconic hero.

M4 World Championship (2022) - Echo

Echo sought to seek revenge from their filipino counterpart Blacklist International after suffering from MPL Season 10 Grand Finals and the M4 Upper Bracket Finals. After taking down the Indonesia powerhouse RRQ Hoshi with a 3 - 1 scoreline, Echo took down the defending champions Blacklist International in the Grand Finals and emerged victorious in a sweep fashion.

Champion Skin: Chou

The skin is dedicated to Echo's roamer Yawi, who shown phenomenal mastery on the hero which strikes fear in every team and the team had a 100% winrate with Yawi on Chou in the M4 World Championship.

M5 World Championship (2023) - AP Bren

After not making to several international stages, AP Bren made a return and made history by becoming the first team to win the M-Series World Championship on home soil and the first ever organisation to win 2 M - Series (M2 as Bren Esports)

Champion Skin: Brody

Brody, the hero played by AP Bren's gold laner Super Marco played a big part in AP Bren's success which also won the team the championship title in the Grand Finals.

Finals MVP Skin: Paquito

Starting from the M5 World championship, Mooton has decided to award a skin to the Finals Mvp

M5 Finals MVP: Flaptzy

Flaptzy has shown remarkable performances on multiple heroes but more notably his Paquito. With multiple game winning plays which led AP Bren to win their 2nd championship title.
