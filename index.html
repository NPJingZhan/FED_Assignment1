About GameWorks Esports

Our History

GameWorks Esports traces its origins to the broader GameWorks brand, established over two decades ago as a hub for video gaming and entertainment. Initially focused on arcade games and immersive family entertainment, the company expanded its vision in response to the rising popularity of esports. Today, GameWorks Esports is a recognized leader in hosting gaming tournaments and events at its venues across the world.

What We Do

GameWorks Esports is a notable name in the world of competitive gaming, hosting both international and local esports tournaments across multiple gaming titles and multiple platforms. Known for organizing events that engage a global audience, GameWorks Esports facilitates competitions in popular games like VALORANT, MLBB, Counter Strike, and League of Legends, among others. These tournaments attract professional teams, aspiring gamers, and enthusiastic viewers from around the globe.

Tournaments

  • OUR BIGGEST TOURNAMENTS

  • VCT Champions Seoul

    The VALORANT Champions Tour 2024 reached its thrilling conclusion in Seoul, Korea, at the world-renowned INSPIRE Arena, where 16 top-tier teams competed for the prestigious title and a $2,250,000 USD prize pool. Broadcasted on all major streaming platforms, the event achieved a record-breaking 1.5 Million peak concurrent viewers, setting a new standard for VALORANT esports and generating a revenue of $35 million USD.

    The atmosphere at INSPIRE Arena was electric, with fans from across the globe witnessing heart-pounding matches between the best teams in VALORANT. VCT Champions Seoul 2024 showcased the high-stakes intensity, strategic depth, and raw talent that have made VALORANT a cornerstone of competitive gaming, leaving a lasting impact on the esports community.

  • M4 World Championship

    The M4 World Championship, held in January 2023, was a landmark event in the Mobile Legends: Bang Bang esports scene. Featuring 16 top teams from around the world, the tournament offered a prize pool of $800,000 USD. The event achieved remarkable viewership milestones, with a peak concurrent audience of over 4 million viewers, making it one of the most-watched esports tournaments globally.

    The championship was broadcast across multiple major streaming platforms, including NimoTV and TikTok Live, significantly expanding its global reach. The total hours watched increased by 27%, the peak viewership increased by 33%, and the average broadcast audience grew by 200,000 people (+33%). This success underscores the growing popularity of mobile esports and the M4 World Championship's pivotal role in the industry.

  • League of Legends Worlds 2024, London

    The League of Legends World Championship 2024 reached an epic conclusion to 2024 LoL Competitive Season in United Kingdom - Korea, at the iconic O2 Arena, where 16 elite teams battled for the Summoner’s Cup and a $3,000,000 USD prize pool. Broadcasted live on all major streaming platforms, the event shattered records with 7 Million peak concurrent viewers, marking a historic moment for esports.

    The energy at the O2 Arena was unparalleled, with thousands of fans coming together to celebrate the pinnacle of competition. Worlds 2024 showcased the unmatched strategy, skill, and passion that make League of Legends a global phenomenon, solidifying its legacy as the premier event in esports.
